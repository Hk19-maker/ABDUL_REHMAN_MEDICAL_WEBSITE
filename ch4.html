Chapter 4

The 17th and 18th centuries brought real scientific change. Anatomy became more accurate. William Harvey explained blood circulation. Hospitals became organized learning spaces. Doctors began observing and documenting their findings with a scientific mindset. Surgery slowly moved from emergency wound care to planned procedures, though it was still limited by the absence of anesthesia and infection control. The scientific attitude of this period prepared the way for the breakthroughs that followed.
